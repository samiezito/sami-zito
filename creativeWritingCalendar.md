# Creative Writing Calendar

The NC State Creative Writing Department requested assistance with the development of several resource management tools. Their idea was to have a space that informed graduate and undergraduate students of professional development opportunities. Before the start of this project, there was no centralized storage location for this information. This required faculty to research, remember, and disseminate information about professional development opportunities to their students. 

Along with my two teammates, we tackled this project in two main sprints. In sprint 1, we compiled a database of creative writing opportunities and designed a spreadsheet for these opportunities. In the second sprint, we refined the spreadsheet, developed a Google Calendar to track deadlines/events, and drafted usage/maintenance documents.

## Tasks Completed

During this project, I thought of myself as the designer. I was responsible for the overall design of the final spreadsheet and the design and creation of the Google Calendar. I wanted to ensure the spreadsheet was relatively simple to use for students, but also that it could be used in view mode (so students do not make any accidental edits). A teammate wrote the instructions for the spreadsheet, but I edited them for simplicity and accuracy. I also served as a project manager and was responsible for creating, assigning, and updating ClickUp tickets.

My partners were responsible for researching relevant opportunities and adding the relevant information into our spreadsheet database.

## Reflection

I was able to showcase my UX design and problem-solving skills through the course of the project. I proposed and implemented the drop-down menus to categorize opportunities and aid filters. I also proposed using a filter view that prevents users from changing the view of the database, but still allows them to filter for relevant professional development opportunities. Finally, I was inspired by my department's calendar to use a color and key code for the Google Calendar so users can quickly identify what kinds of opportunities have upcoming deadlines. 
I also developed my project management skills by creating and managing ClickUp tickets. I have never been the PM for a project and have only updated tickets with comments. Creating them and understanding what the tasks and subtasks were was a new experience and highlighted the areas where more practice is needed.
